Applications of Fan SmartParts

The Fan SmartPart can be used to model axial or rectangular fans which can be configured to simulate a variety of designs.

Axial Fans

An axial fan is represented by building any required base, cowling, and hub out of cuboids and prisms, according to the level of detail required.

The fan blades are not individually modeled, only the expected flow passing through the region they occupy is required. Any expected swirl may also be included.

Tubeaxial Fans

A tubeaxial fan can be modeled by locating a fan in a hole in a cuboid (Block with Hole SmartPart), see Figure 1.

Centrifugal Fans

You can create a centrifugal fan by combining a minimum of four axial fans and a collapsed cuboid backplate, see Figure 2.

Figure 2. Centrifugal Fan

You can also create a centrifugal fan using the Blower Macro, available as a utility from the Simcenter Flotherm Related Downloads page on Support Center.

Combinations of Fans

More complex arrangements can be built up by the introduction of several fans, for example, fans in parallel and fans in series.

Fans in series must be separated by at least one grid cell, see Figure 3.

Figure 3. Fans in Parallel and in Series

Fan Locations

Fans can be located:

  • On the domain boundary, blowing air into, or extracting air from, the domain.

    Figure 4. Fans on the Domain Boundary

  • Totally within the domain, stimulating fluid motion by imparting momentum to the fluid.

    Figure 5. Fans Within the Domain

  • Fans can be put inside the domain when they are connected on one side with a cutout, as illustrated in Figure 6.

    Figure 6. Fan on Cutout

Transient Attributes Attached to Fans

It is possible to attach a Transient attribute to a fan. In such cases the Transient attribute multiplier is applied to the derating factor of the fan.

Modeling the Effects of Finger Guards or Grilles

When an axial fan is located on the domain boundary, by default the program assumes a zero loss resistance. To model the effects of finger guards or grilles, attach a resistance to the fan. This option only works if the fan has a 3D modeling level.
