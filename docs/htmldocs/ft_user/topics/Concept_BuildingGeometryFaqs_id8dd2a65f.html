Building Geometry FAQs

Answers to frequently asked questions (FAQs) about geometry.

  1. Q: What are assemblies and how do they work?

    A: Assemblies are collections of geometry shapes grouped together to create functional units. Grouping geometry enables a structural approach to creating geometry and makes it easier to debug complex shapes.

    The complete geometry structure is held in the top (Root) assembly, which can be divided into sub-assemblies as shown in Figure 1.

    Figure 1. Root Assembly and Sub-Assemblies

    Each assembly is located via a reference origin and can contain primitives, SmartParts, and other assemblies, as well as regions and monitor points. The assembly geometry is located relative to assembly origin, so moving an assembly will move all the constituent parts by the same amount.

    See “Creating, Importing, and Exporting Geometry” for a description of the geometry model.
