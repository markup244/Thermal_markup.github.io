Advice on SmartParts and Orthotropic Conduction

If applying orthotropic conductivity to SmartParts, then decompose the SmartPart and let the local coordinates Xo, Yo, and Zo be displayed.

The kx, ky, kz settings in the Material property sheet are applied to the Xo, Yo, and Zo directions respectively (Figure 1).

Figure 1. Orthotropic Conductivity in an Enclosure

In Figure 1 an enclosure is decomposed. ky would be the normal (through-plane) conductivity for the upper wall, but would be in-plane for the right wall. If it is really intended to work with orthotropic conductivities in the walls, then some more materials have to be defined with proper permutations of kx, ky, and kz.

CAUTION:

Strange results will arise from unintended attachment of orthotropic conductivities to SmartParts.
