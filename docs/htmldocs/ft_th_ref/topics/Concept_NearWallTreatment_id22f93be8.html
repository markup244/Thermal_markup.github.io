Near Wall Treatment

Corrections are made to the turbulent viscosity so as to damp its value from the bulk value outside the wall boundary layer to zero at the wall surface. Fluid velocity at a wall surface is zero, which is known as the no-slip condition. The type of flow between the wall and the bulk flow is known as a shear layer, in this case a wall boundary layer.

The boundary layer is a very complex region of high velocity gradient and diffusion dominated development. To model it precisely would necessitate an extremely fine grid. An empirical relationship is therefore used to describe the shape of the boundary layer so that only one grid cell near the wall is required.
