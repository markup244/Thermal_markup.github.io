General Effect of Localizing a Grid

Localizing a grid can be an effective way of reducing the number of cells.

Figure 1 shows the effect of localizing the grid for an isolated cuboid. The ten grid constraint lines inside the cuboid on the right are not needed in the air outside it, therefore, a grid space has been defined by the cuboid such that only the keypoints defining the edges of the grid space continue to the edges of the solution domain.

This is the simplest case, localizing a grid may be affected by other grid modifications and nearby geometry.
