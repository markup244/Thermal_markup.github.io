Modeling a Remote Radiation Source

The heat loss/gain through radiative transfer is achieved by setting the remote source temperature in the Model Setup tab and then applying an emissivity value to the exterior surface using a Surface attribute.

Within a Room

An example of its use is an enclosure within a room with walls at a constant temperature as shown in Figure 1.

Figure 1. Modeling a Remote Radiation Source

In this case there is radiant exchange between the enclosure walls and the remote surfaces.

Setting the external radiant temperature enables the net radiant flux to the surface to be calculated from the following expression:

σFε ((Tremote)4 − (Twall)4)

where:

  • σ is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant.

  • F is the view factor.

  • ε is the emissivity of the surface.

  • Tremote is the temperature of the remote surface, which is distinct from the bulk temperature of the fluid surrounding the enclosure.

  • Twall is the calculated temperature of the external surface of the enclosure (not available to the user).

External Ambient at Night

For an external cabinet at night, the external radiant temperature is that of the sky. This will be dependent on the local conditions, cloud cover, and so on.
