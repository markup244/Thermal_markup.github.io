External Angled Resistance

When external angled resistance is modeled, the resistance uses the direction cosines of the flow coming into the enclosure.

This is illustrated for the X-Y plane in Figure 1.

Figure 1. Direction of Angled Resistance

The x-direction cosine is Cos(θ).

The y-direction cosine is Cos(π/2 − θ) = Sin(θ).

For example, property sheet settings of XoN = 0, YoN = 1 and ZoN = 1 are normalized to 0, 1/√2 and 1/√2, that is, 0, Cos 45° and Sin 45°.

The program checks the positive/negative sense of the direction and always sets it to conform with the inward normal.
