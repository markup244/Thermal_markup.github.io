Fixed Volume Flow

For an intake fan on the domain boundary, the fluid is drawn in from the external environment. By default, the fluid brought in this way is at the ambient temperature applied to the sides of the overall system domain using the Ambient attributes.

By default, the program calculates the axial momentum (that is, velocity) of the incoming fluid from Fan volume flow rate per unit area.

This is the velocity normal to the plane of the fan. However, it is possible to allow for the effect of an inclined flow by setting the Supply direction angle.

For exhaust fans (that is, outlets), the situation is quite different, the fluid leaving the enclosure leaves it with the conditions and properties that the fluid has on the enclosure side of the fan. Therefore, the temperature of the fluid leaving the enclosure through an exhaust fan is the temperature calculated by the program adjacent to the exit. The fluid leaves the enclosure with whatever angle the program computes for the flow towards the fan.
